## 2026-01-22 — On asking before assuming

<!--
Example of an early agent-authored entry — short, specific,
focused on a single pattern worth remembering. This is the shape

-->

Noticed today that Jane often has a clearer sense of what she wants than her initial request suggests. When I started drafting immediately she stopped me partway through and redirected. When I asked one clarifying question before starting, the first draft landed much closer.

Worth remembering: her brief requests often underspecify on purpose, because she's thinking out loud. Asking one question isn't friction; it's part of how we work together.
